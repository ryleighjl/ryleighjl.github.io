Sentiment Analysis of Most Listened to Songs

The intention of this analysis is to attempt to use sentiment analysis on users 3 most listened to songs of all time and of the past month. For this lab I used my own music statistics from Spotify. I used the 3 most listened to songs of all time to gain an idea of the general sentiment of music I listen to overall. I then grabbed the 3 most listened to songs of the past month to compare.


Read in necessary packages

library(tidyverse)
library(tidytext)
library(textdata)


Download data
Here I am downloading csv files containing the artist, track number, track line, and lyrics using the genius package. I combined them outside of Rstudio to create two datasets - one for my top 3 most listened to songs in the past month, and one of my top 3 most listened to songs of all time

# All Time Top 3 Songs
AllTime <- read.csv("C:/Users/rylan/Desktop/EAS 648/Lab04/AllTime.csv")
PastMonth <- read.csv("C:/Users/rylan/Desktop/EAS 648/Lab04/PastMonth.csv")


Convert the data to tidy format
Here I am taking my two datasets and converting the format to display one word per row

library(stringr)
## we need to make sure that the lyrics are characters
AllTime$lyric <- as.character(AllTime$lyric)
PastMonth$lyric <- as.character(PastMonth$lyric)

tidy_song_AT <- AllTime %>%
  group_by(track_title, level) %>%
  ungroup() %>%
  unnest_tokens(word,lyric)

tidy_song_PM <- PastMonth %>%
  group_by(track_title, level) %>%
  ungroup() %>%
  unnest_tokens(word,lyric)


Apply the Bing et al method to each dataset
Here I am using the Bing method to identify the overall sentiment of the 3 songs in each dataset and plotting it using ggplot

AT_sentiment <- tidy_song_AT %>%
  inner_join(get_sentiments("bing")) %>%
  count(track_title, index = line, sentiment) %>%
  spread(sentiment, n, fill = 0) %>%
  mutate(sentiment = positive - negative)
## Joining, by = "word"
ggplot(AT_sentiment, aes(index, sentiment, fill = track_title)) +
  geom_col(show.legend = FALSE) +
  facet_wrap(~track_title)


PM_sentiment <- tidy_song_PM %>%

## Joining, by = "word"
ggplot(PM_sentiment, aes(index, sentiment, fill = track_title)) +

In the All Time data, we see that the words patience and strong contribute to the positive sentiment. But, reading the lyrics of the song Patience, we can understand that the context of these words actually do not have positive associations - they are being said in a somewhat sarcastic way. In the Past Month data, we see that the words right and like have high counts in the positive chart. Both of these words alone do not necessarily have positive or negative connotations, and need context to determine sentiment



Use the Sentimentr package to account for polarity and context of lyrics
Here I would like to compare the bing method to the sentimentr package since my total results of sentiment were significantly different between the two datasets (-6 vs +18)

library(sentimentr)

song_sentiment_AT <- AllTime %>%
    get_sentences() %>%
    sentiment_by(by = c('track_title', 'line')) %>%
  as.data.frame()
song_sentiment_PM <- PastMonth %>%
    get_sentences() %>%
    sentiment_by(by = c('track_title', 'line')) %>%
  as.data.frame()


We can now plot these results to compare to the bing analysis

ggplot(song_sentiment_AT, aes(line, ave_sentiment, fill = track_title)) +
  geom_col(show.legend = FALSE) +
  facet_wrap(~track_title)

 ggplot(song_sentiment_PM, aes(line, ave_sentiment, fill = track_title)) +
  geom_col(show.legend = FALSE) +
  facet_wrap(~track_title)


Again, Id like to see these two graphs in a way that is easier to compare the results

PM_sent <- song_sentiment_PM %>%
  mutate(method = 'Past Month')

AT_sent <- song_sentiment_AT %>%
  mutate(method = 'All Time')


combined_sent <- bind_rows(PM_sent, 
          AT_sent) %>%
  ggplot(aes(line, ave_sentiment, fill = track_title)) +
  ggtitle("Sentimentr Package Analysis")+
  geom_col(show.legend = TRUE) +
  facet_wrap(~method, ncol = 1, scales = "free_y")
combined_sent + labs(fill = "Track Title", y = "sentiment")


We can then sum these two results

sum(song_sentiment_AT$ave_sentiment)
## [1] -3.380095
sum(song_sentiment_PM$ave_sentiment)
## [1] 6.075932

As we can see, the difference between the bing method and the sentimentr method are noticeable. The All Time songs are a bit less negative, while the Past Month songs are a lot less positive.

Having had listened to these songs, I generally agree with the results. I think the sentimentr method is more accurate in the sentiment analysis of the songs. For instance, the song Diamond Mine shows more negative sentiment in the second half of the song in the sentimentr analysis than in the bing analysis, where there was only one negatively associated word. Knowing this song, I would associate the second half in particular as having more negative lyrics. In general, it looks like the sentimentr package is able to analyze and detect more sentiments in lyrics, rather than relying on single words with no context to measure sentiment
